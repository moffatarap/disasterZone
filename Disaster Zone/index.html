About

Concept Details

Disaster Zone is a single-page responsive website that brings together information on disasters occurring in the world in real-time to one easy to access website and mobile application. Disaster Zone must scale to all modern devices including desktops, laptops, tablets and mobile devices. Both the website and the mobile application run in tandem, displaying the same info at all times. Users of the website or the mobile application will be notified of disasters near them when required after a sign-up process. Disaster Zone uses GPS location data along with postcodes and city addresses for the alerts process, if a disaster event is near a user. The user is informed by SMS, Email or in-app push notification telling them of event and what to do. Users can post warnings about events in their area via the app or website these event notifications can be seen by other users and staff of Disaster Zone in the Google maps API.

More Info

All events are quality controlled to make sure they are valid and that the data set is correct and up to date. Disaster Zone acts as an information site to help people in the event of a disaster and to coordinate volunteers in the aftermath of a natural disaster. The other function of is the Disaster Zone relief fund; user-donated money to support people in a disaster zone. No profit is taken from donations, all funds go directly to where itsâ€™ needed. The website is made for all ages; it is designed with simplicity and user ability in mind. Someone with very little technical skills needs to be able to use and operate the website easily and effectively in a time of stress.

Disaster Zone is a concept made by Arapaoa Moffat started in project two of MDDN201 2015, and is going to be carried on in development though the duration of MDDN352 2016.

Disaster Zone Severity Scale

  1. Severe level of damage to people and or property, high risk of injury and human casualties. 10,000+ people missing or injured 70% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
  2. Strong level of damage to people and or property, medium high risk of injury and human casualties. 5,000 to 8,000 people missing or injured 60% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
  3. Moderate level of damage to people and or property, medium risk of injury and human casualties. 500 to 1,000 people missing or injured 30% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
  1. Light level of damage to people and or property, low risk of injury and human casualties. 0 to 100 people missing or injured 0% to 15% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
  2. Weak to no damage to people and or property, low to risk of injury and human casualties. 0 people missing or injured 0% to 5% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
